Third-Party Apps

Jack wanted to play Pokemon Go before it was released in the Google Play Store. He downloaded a version that was available online.

He couldn't find the app in the phone after installation. Instead, Jack found an app called "PI Network."

Curious, Jack opened it up. His phone froze immediately, and Jack was forced to shut his phone off by removing the battery.

The app was nowhere to be found, but nows ads pop up all the time.


Smartphones allow you to install applications from third-party sources through a process called side-loading. Third-party apps offer more variety and can sometimes provide earlier releases than an official app store. Official app stores screen their apps, making it less likely for developers to embed malware or adware into their app.

App Permissions

Have you ever downloaded an app that requested your permission to access your private data?

When you install some apps, they ask for your permission to access your datas. These permissions can include anything from your location data to accessing your contacts. Some apps can even send text messages and calls.

Some apps can misuse your personal information. Here are some things that apps can access provided the right permissions:

  • Names and phone numbers in your contact list.
  • Text message, emails, and files on your phone.
  • Location data, which can be used to determine where you live, work, or go to school.
  • Photos.
  • Microphone and camera, including turning them on whenever.
What should you do?
  • Make sure that the required permissions make sense.
  • Research apps that require permissions that seem strange to verify legitimacy.
