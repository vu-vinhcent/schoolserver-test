What is phishing?

A malicious entity contacts you, masquerading as a legitimate business and asks for information, directs you to a false website that tries to obtain your information, or otherwise tries to exploit you.

Phishing Warning Signs
  • Requests for personal information
  • Generic greeting
  • Urgency
  • False links
Fake Websites

Phishing emails can take you to fake websites that are designed to look like legitimate websites.

How do you identify fake websites?
  • Check the URL! URLs are unique, so no two websites can have the same URL. However, www.google.com and www.goog1e.com can look a lot alike, especially at a glance.
  • Check whether the website is using HTTPS or HTTP. Most websites that involve secure transactions or your personal information will use HTTPS.
  • Sometimes, websites use special fonts, which cannot be easily replicated. Watch out for fonts that look out of place, like this part of the sentence.
  • Obvious grammar errors are also a suspicious sign. Very rarely will there be grammar errors on genuine websites, especially ones for businesses.
Spear Phishing

"Spear Phishing" is a specific type of phishing attack that appears to come from a trusted source. Attackers can use information they find online to impersonate someone that the victim trusts, increasing the chance of a successful attack.

Privacy

Disclosing personal information on the internet can have devastating effects. Social networks can provide attackers with information such as full name, DOB, hometown, relationship information, education, graduation dates, pet names, interests, hobbies, and affiliations. This information can be used in a spear phishing attack to gain further information about a target, as well as crafting targeted password attacks.

There is a shocking amount of information available online. Knowing someone's name and location is often all you need to find specific details about someone. Type your first and last name into the form below to see if we can find information about you.

Some tips to keep your information safe and out of the hands of attackers:
  • Be mindful of how much personal information you share on social networks.
  • Dispose of old computers and phones (any device with data) securely.
  • For password security verification questions, use a password for all answers. The answers to most security questions are things that an attacker can find out about you.
